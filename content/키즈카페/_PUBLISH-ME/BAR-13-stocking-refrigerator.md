## OBJECTIVE
Associates know how to **stock refrigerator** properly.

---

## PROCEDURE
- All beverage items must be **cleaned and free from dust and stains**.
- **Damaged items** should not be placed in the refrigerator — exchange at the beverage store for a new item.
- **Wines, champagnes must be stored horizontal** to prevent corks drying out.
- Popular & fast-moving items should be **well stocked**.
- **Juices** placed at the **bottom tier** to prevent drips on other items. All containers **sealed** while stored.
- Always check shelves are **strong enough**; **do not overload** the fridge.
- Keep **similar items together**, stack same items behind each other.
- Keep a **"par record"** of cans/bottles in a fridge to help counting during closing of stocks.
- When replenishing, **old stock to the front, new items to the back** (FIFO).
- If the fridge is small, **replenish constantly** to maintain sufficient chilled beverages.
- **Garnishes** in sealed containers, within easy reach.
- **Cream, honey, milk** in airtight containers or closed with plastic cling wrappers.
- **Never block the blowers/fan** — ensure space for cold air to circulate.
