Associates know how to stock refrigerator properly

PROCEDURE DESCRIPTION   
  

All beverage items must be cleaned and free from dust and stains.

Damaged items should not be placed in the refrigerator but should be exchanged at the beverage store for a new item.

Wines, champagnes must be stored horizontal to prevent their corks drying out.

Popular and fast moving should be well stocked in the refrigerator.

Juices must be placed at the bottom tier of the fridge to prevent drips on other items in the fridge. All containers should be sealed while being stored inside the fridge.

Always check that the shelves are strong enough to hold the weight. Do not overload fridge.

Keep similar items together and stack same items behind each other.

Keep a “par record” on the amount of can or bottle drinks in a fridge to help the counting process during closing of stocks.

When replenishing beverages, ensure that the old beverage stocks are taken out from the back of the refrigerator and Then new items in its place.

If the fridge is small, replenish the fridge constantly to maintain sufficient chilled beverages.

Garnishes must be kept in sealed containers, within easy reach of the associates.

Cream, honey, milk is to be kept in airtight containers or closed with plastic cling wrappers.

Never block the blowers/fan of the refrigerator. Ensure there is enough space for the blower to circulate cold air around the fridge.
