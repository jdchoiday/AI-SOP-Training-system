1.  **\*\*When you play with kids\*\***

When playground staff plays with kids, it's important to be aware of certain things to avoid. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1.  Rough or aggressive play: Avoid engaging in rough or aggressive play that could potentially harm the children or encourage them to engage in unsafe behaviors.

2.  Inappropriate physical contact: Refrain from any form of inappropriate physical contact with the children. Maintain appropriate boundaries and adhere to professional standards.

3.  Favoritism: Avoid showing favoritism towards specific children. Treat all children equally and provide opportunities for everyone to participate and enjoy the activities.

4.  Excluding or isolating children: Ensure that all children feel included and have the opportunity to participate in the activities. Avoid excluding or isolating any child from the group.

5.  Use of inappropriate language or behavior: Maintain a professional and appropriate demeanor when interacting with the children. Avoid using offensive language or engaging in inappropriate behavior.

6.  Ignoring safety guidelines: Follow all safety guidelines and procedures set for the playground. Avoid taking unnecessary risks or neglecting safety measures during playtime.

7.  Overstepping boundaries: Respect the boundaries and personal space of the children. Avoid invading their personal space or engaging in activities that may make them uncomfortable.

8.  Disregarding rules and regulations: Ensure that both staff and children follow the rules and regulations of the playground. Model appropriate behavior and enforce the rules consistently.

9.  Neglecting supervision: Stay active in supervising the children to prevent accidents or injuries. Avoid distractions that may compromise your ability to supervise effectively.

10.  Lack of communication: Maintain open and transparent communication with the children. Avoid ignoring their needs, concerns, or requests. Listen actively and address any issues that arise during playtime.

By being aware of these things to avoid, you can create a safe, inclusive, and enjoyable environment for the children during playtime.

2.  **\*\*When you talk with kids\*\***

When playground staff are talking with kids, it's important to be mindful of certain things to avoid. Here are some guidelines to keep in mind:

1.  Talking down to children: Avoid patronizing or talking down to children. Treat them with respect and use age-appropriate language and tone when communicating with them.

2.  Ignoring or dismissing their concerns: Take children's concerns, questions, and ideas seriously. Avoid dismissing or ignoring their thoughts or feelings. Listen actively and provide thoughtful responses.

3.  Using inappropriate language or topics: Maintain professional and appropriate language when speaking with children. Avoid using profanity, offensive language, or discussing topics that are not suitable for their age group.

4.  Dominating the conversation: Give children the opportunity to express themselves and contribute to the conversation. Avoid dominating or overpowering the discussion. Encourage active participation and listen attentively.

5.  Interrupting or not actively listening: Show respect by actively listening to what children have to say. Avoid interrupting them or not giving them a chance to fully express their thoughts or ideas.

6.  Neglecting non-verbal cues: Pay attention to children's non-verbal cues, such as body language or facial expressions. Be sensitive to their emotions and adjust your communication accordingly.

7.  Overstepping personal boundaries: Respect children's personal boundaries and avoid prying into their personal lives or asking invasive questions. Maintain a professional and appropriate level of interaction.

8.  Neglecting confidentiality: Keep any personal information shared by the children confidential unless it raises concerns about their safety or well-being. Respect their privacy and maintain trust.

9.  Using aggressive or intimidating behavior: Avoid using aggressive or intimidating behavior when speaking with children. Be patient, calm, and supportive in your interactions. Create a safe and nurturing environment.

10.  Dismissing their perspectives or opinions: Value children's perspectives and opinions. Avoid dismissing or undermining their ideas or beliefs. Encourage them to express themselves and engage in meaningful conversations.

*By being mindful of these guidelines, playground staff can foster effective and respectful communication with children, building trust and positive relationships.*
